Bone Collections

In order to sort bones together for clarity, the rig has sorted them into bone collections. In the Properties Area, you can view the Bone Collections in the Data Tab.

Bones are sorted in these general categories:

  1. Body

    1. IK-Bones

      Inverse Kinematic and other Driver Bones for main body parts (e.g.: arms/legs, spine, head, etc.)

    2. Moveable Bones

      Forward Kinematic Bones for either main body parts (e.g.: arms/legs) or especially extra body parts (e.g.: hair/floof, fingers, etc.)

    3. Static Bones

      PLEASE REFRAIN FROM DOING ANYTHING IN THIS COLLECTION. It can result into unwanted results. These bones are controlled by drivers and constraints.

  2. Face

    1. FaceRig

      PLEASE REFRAIN FROM DOING ANYTHING IN THIS COLLECTION. It can result into unwanted results. These facial bones are controlled by drivers and constraints.

    2. Facial Handlers

      Forward Kinematics Bones for the face

      1. Facial Mouth

      2. Facial Eyes

(Categories can vary on features implemented!)

You can expand or shrink collections like folders to make it more detailed/see inside collections or condensed/see header group as you want.

In the Bone Collections section you can toggle the visibility in the Viewport.

Either you toggle visibility through

  • Visible icon

    for general visibility settings

  • Solo icon

    for picking out single collections

Inverse vs. Forward Kinematics

In Rigs you will encounter the topic of Kinematics. The main two ones you will definitely encounter is the question of using Forward or Inverse Kinematics. It is important to know the basics of these two ways of movement beforehand. Additionally this manual will point out, where and how the kinematics can be used for the rigs.

Forward Kinematics (FK)

Basics:

Imagine waving your arm, swinging your legs forward and backward like a pendulum or just giving someone a good punching hook. Most of these motions use arch-like movements commonly, which falls into forward kinematics.

FK uses an hierarchical system, in which the rotation of a parent bone affects the child bones position and rotation.

For example: Imagine three points to visualize on your arm: Shoulder, Elbow, Hand. If you wave your arm, the shoulder begins to rotate, this will move the elbow's and, along with that, the hand's position. However, rotating your hand alone does not move the elbow, let alone the shoulder.

Makes sense: The hand is connected to the elbow, which is connected to the shoulder. So every movement/rotation of the shoulder affects every other parts of the limb. Not vice versa! Meaning a hierachy exists:

  1. Shoulder

  2. Elbow

  3. Hand

This is the core concept, important to keep in mind! Use FK, when you want to pose/animate an arch-like movement.

Inverse Kinematics (IK)

Basics:

With FK alone, the process of posing is going to be tedious. Remember you need to pose a limb down a hierarchy chain, meaning slight changes in parent bones affects all the child bones. Frustrating when you have to align something to a specific place. If there was something that inverses the order of the hierarchy...

Imagine reaching something with a hand, moving your foot directly up or throwing a straight punch. These movements use targets to move a certain part of the body in a specific location, while the rest of the limb adapts with the motion accordingly.

IK allows you to use the last bone in the hierarchy and intuitively/efficiently move it to a desired location (while the other bones of the limbs calculate their angles to adjust to that bone). That renders the hierarchy of the FK invalid, which in most rigs means, that you can't rotate the parent bones that are affected by IK.

In order to give more control in the rotation of the limb, we have pole bones.

Switch between IK / FK

Now that you know what IK and FK is (or at least have gotten the gist of it), you need to know how to toggle between them.

Go to the Properties of the Rig (Select RIGPROPERTIES BONE > Sidebar > Item > Properties). There you can individually toggle between the IK (value: 0) and FK (value: 1) of the part of the rig.

  • L IK/FK ARM: Left Arm's IK/FK Toggle

  • L IK/FK LEG: Left Leg's IK/FK Toggle

  • R IK/FK ARM: Right Arm's IK/FK Toggle

  • R IK/FK LEG: Right Leg's IK/FK Toggle

Tail Rig Values

If your model has a tail, then most probably the rig has a tail rig and additionally mechanisms for smooth tail waggling.

  • Tail Damped Amount: Damping Amount of the tail's rig

    • 0 means tail behaves stiff and completely follows the root's rotation

    • 1 means tail behaves loose and completely neglegts the root's rotation

  • Tail Damped IK: Percentage of how much the tail follows the IK and lessens the tail damp

    • 0 means tail will follow the IK and tail damp will be added on top of it

      • 1 means tail will strictly follow the IK and tail damp is no more



  • Tail IK/FK: Tail's IK/FK Toggle

In order to utilize the tail damp you only need to rotate the tail's root bone (you can also rotate the other parts if you so desire)

Pose Library

Some Rigs have poses stored in a Pose Library. In order to access them you need to go to the Asset Browser window. (Shortcut: [Shift] + [F1])

Usually if there is a pose library, only face poses are saved there.

In order to use them you need to select the wanted bones that needs changes. Best practice is to select bones from the corresponding bone collections.

Select each of the Collection you want to select (make sure they are visible) and press the Select button, then you can double click the pose in the Asset Browser and the selected bones of the rig will copy the saved pose asset.
